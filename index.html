Currently, Kate investigates tissue and organ patterning mechanisms in the model plant Arabidopsis. Kate optimized plant culture, confocal imaging, image processing and gene expression quantification techniques to this end. She will be completing her Ph.D. on this work at Cornell University in the summer of 2022.

Kate actively engages in community building and outreach as a graduate resident fellow for upperclassmen students, through yearly science workshops she designs and hosts for aspiring women and minorities in STEM and regular participation in science communication, diversity and inclusivity training. In her "free" time, Kate enjoys practicing yoga and dance, skiing, backpacking and running.

Kate is a recipient of the National Science Foundation Graduate Research Felloship, Presidential Life Sciences Fellowship, Ian D.W. Cramer Prize for Dance, Stanley Spector Memorial Award (Essay), Donald Danforth Plant Science Center REU Fellowship and Tyson Research Center Fellowship.
